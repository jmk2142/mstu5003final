Thinking about Consultation

Consultation is a process of simply finding out the views and perspectives of your consumers. Often the dialogue that is created through the consultation process provides insight into the needs, concerns and opinions of consumers. Nonprofits often use this information to make programmatic or policy decisions. Consultation can take many forms ranging from focus groups to lengthy needs assessments. Consultation activities are one-time time events often limited to one day or a single interaction with an individual or group. Non-profits may plan consultation activities annually or at regular intervals throughout the course of the year, however most non-profits will not consult with the same group of individuals twice during that time. This may be different for longer-term programs and we will discuss briefly on-going consultation with static groups. Consultation differs from Consumer Involvement, which will be discussed later in this course.
